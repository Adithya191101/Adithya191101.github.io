Electric Three Wheeler - Campus Solar Panel Transporter

A reverse-engineered electric utility vehicle designed in SOLIDWORKS for transporting solar panels around the college campus. The design has been implemented and is currently in use at PSG College of Technology.

December 2021 - February 2022
PSG College of Technology

Electric Vehicle Design

Project Overview

This project involved reverse-engineering an existing three-wheeler vehicle and redesigning it as an electric utility vehicle for transporting solar panels around the college campus. The team physically measured an actual three-wheeler, analyzed its dimensions and structure, and modified the design to accommodate an electric powertrain.

Extensive field research was conducted to identify suitable motors capable of handling the required payload. The research resulted in motor recommendations that can pull up to 1.3 tons of payload, ensuring the vehicle can reliably transport heavy solar panel installations across the campus terrain.

Successfully Implemented

This design has been implemented and is currently operational at PSG College of Technology, serving as a practical solution for campus solar panel transportation needs.

Design & Visualization

The vehicle was designed using SOLIDWORKS with detailed 3D models created for all components including the frame, suspension, steering mechanism, and cargo bed assembly.

Vehicle Architecture

The three-wheeler utilizes a tricycle configuration with a single front steering wheel and dual rear drive wheels, providing excellent stability while maintaining a compact footprint for navigating narrow campus pathways.

Chassis System

  • Square tube steel frame construction
  • Modular design for easy maintenance
  • Optimized weight distribution
  • Rust-resistant coating

Steering System

  • Single front wheel configuration
  • Ackermann steering geometry
  • Handlebar-style controls
  • Tight turning radius capability

Cargo System

  • Hydraulic cylinder-operated tilting bed
  • Solar panel securing brackets
  • Padded contact surfaces
  • Easy load/unload mechanism

Suspension System

  • Fox 40 fork front suspension
  • Leaf spring rear suspension
  • Pneumatic wheelbarrow wheels
  • Vibration dampening for cargo

Design Process

The project followed a systematic reverse-engineering approach combined with field research to create an optimized electric vehicle design.

1

Physical Measurement

The team physically measured an existing three-wheeler vehicle, documenting all critical dimensions, structural components, and mechanical assemblies for accurate CAD reproduction.

2

Design Modification

The original design was modified in SOLIDWORKS to accommodate electric vehicle components, including motor mounts, battery housing, and an optimized cargo bed for solar panel transport.

3

Motor Field Research

Extensive field research was conducted to identify and recommend suitable electric motors. The selected motors are capable of pulling up to 1.3 tons of payload, ensuring reliable performance for heavy solar panel loads.

4

Implementation

The final design was approved and implemented at PSG College of Technology, where it now serves as an operational vehicle for campus solar panel transportation.
